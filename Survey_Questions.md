# End-of-Course Survey Instrument

1 = Strongly disagree, 2 = Disagree, 3 = Neither agree nor disagree, 4 = Agree, 5 = Strongly agree

Statements are grouped by module (M1-M6) and correspond to questions Q1-Q12. The survey was administered through Moodle. 10 out of 24 active groups responded.

---

## M1: Process Identification and AI Agents

**Q1.** Building an AI agent in Module 1 provided our group with an early, hands-on understanding of how AI agents can be integrated into business processes.

**Q2.** Reflecting on the role of the AI agent from Module 1 helped our group understand how AI components can contribute across the full BPM lifecycle.

---

## M2: Process Discovery and Workflow Pattern Analysis

**Q3.** Constructing the as-is process model helped our group develop a shared, precise understanding of the case, including its control flow and resource interactions.

**Q4.** Evaluating the workflow patterns supported by n8n helped our group systematically assess the expressive capabilities and limitations of workflow-based execution engines.

---

## M3: Process Analysis, Simulation, and Redesign

**Q5.** Simulating the as-is process in WoPeD enabled our group to establish a quantitative performance baseline for the current process.

**Q6.** Simulating alternative to-be configurations in WoPeD allowed our group to pre-evaluate and compare the expected impact of different automation and redesign choices before committing to them.

**Q7.** Analysing the performance spectrum helped our group identify additional redesign patterns from simulated event logs that were not apparent from the model structure alone.

---

## M4: Process Redesign Using BPMN

**Q8.** Formalising the redesigned process in BPMN allowed our group to represent it in an industry-standard notation that is sufficiently precise for implementation.

---

## M5: Workflow Implementation and AI Agent Construction

**Q9.** Implementing the process in n8n gave our group concrete experience of how AI-augmented processes can be enacted in a real workflow platform.

**Q10.** Building the RAG-based fraud-detection agent helped our group understand how Retrieval-Augmented Generation can be applied within an operational process context.

---

## M6: Process Mining

**Q11.** Working through the process mining module gave our group practical experience of discovering process models from multi-year historical event logs exhibiting concept drift.

**Q12.** The process mining module deepened our group's understanding of conformance checking and the interpretation of fitness and precision metrics for discovered models.
